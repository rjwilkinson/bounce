Configuring User Permission

Learn how to assign permissions to users so they can help manage your surveys and results

Navigation
Posted 15/01/2020 @Bob Wilkinson

Introduction

You can configure the permissions of each user in Bounce, which is really useful when you want to give other members of your staff permission to do certain things... but not everything! For example, you may want to give a teacher the ability to see the results for a specific year group but not all year groups.

When you first set Bounce up in your school, everyone will be a 'user' (with the exception of the administrator) which is a group that has very limited access. The steps below will explain more of how to assign extra privileges/rights to your users.

Log in as Administrator

Before you can view the permissions menu, you will need to log in as a user that has 'administrative' access. Ensure you are logged in as this user before continuing!

When you are, click Tools / User Permissions from the top menu.

Configuring Permissions

Once you are logged in, you will be able to see all the groups that have been created within your organisation and can start configuring the permissions for designated users.

  1. If you are delegating permissions to other members of staff within your school, you will want to select your Organisation followed by the STAFF group.
  2. Next, choose the access right you want to assign to each user. There are 3 to choose from:

    User. The default group with very limited permission. Users in this group can log in to Bounce to participate in surveys they have been assigned (and see ones they have completed previously) but cannot do much else.

    Staff. The Staff group is the 'next level up'. Users in this group can assign/create surveys and see the results for surveys of which they have been granted year group permission.

    Administrator. This group has full unrestricted access to the management of Bounce in your school. Even the administrator can participate in a survey however!



    Select the desired access right next to their name.

  3. Now you should select which year groups you want to grant access to. You can do this by clicking the appropriate year groups next to the user's name.

    In the example above, 2 year groups have been chosen. This means that the selected user will be able to manage those year groups when they log in. This includes the assigning of surveys and viewing the results. If you want to grant them access to more year groups, click to select those one as well.

  4. Once you have made the changes to your users, scroll to the bottom of the page and click Update Permission to save the changes you have specified.

That's it! next time that user logs in, they will have access as you have defined above.
